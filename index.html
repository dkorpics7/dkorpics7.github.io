My Journey

A New Start

When I was seven years old, my best friend and I would sit in my closet and make math problems for each other to solve! Consequently, it is no surprise that we grew up to be engineers. I loved my job as an engineer primarily because it involved lots of FORTRAN programming and software support. When I had children, I stayed home to raise them and ultimately homeschooled them through high school. During this time, I performed all the financial duties for my father's small corporation and taught high school math and science at a private school. Now that my children are grown, I have decided to go back to what I loved most ... programming. Thus, I am currently enrolled in the We Can Code IT bootcamp to update my coding skills. I have "worn many hats" during my life, and you can read about them in more detail by clicking on the titles below.

Reservoir Engineer

My job in the Research and Development Group at British Petroleum was very rewarding. I was the liason between the developers and the users of a complex reservoir simulator (VIP). I also used the model to predict oil and gas production from the Prudhoe Bay reservoir in Alaska. Besides helping and training the users, my favorite part of the job was writing FORTRAN utilities to enable the simulator to work with third-party pre-and post-processors in order to improve efficiency.

Homeschool Mom

Homeschooling my children was my most challenging job. Besides juggling the roles of mother, teacher, and wife, combining the right balance of academic challenge, extra-curricular activities, and hands-on experiences was difficult. Selecting curriculums, creating lesson plans, scheduling classes and activities, providing assessments for the state, teaching as part of coop groups, and volunteering to run debate tournaments were just some among many of my responsibilities. I like to think that I was successful based on the accomplishments of my children. Two of the three received college merit scholarships and graduated Summa Cum Laude from their colleges. The other is currently finishing up a two-year associate degree in Accounting Technology after being adopted at age 14 with no English language skills and having been paralyzed several months before the adoption was final.

Math Teacher

While homeschooling my children, I was recruited by a local, private school to teach math and science to high-school students. As much as I love mathematics, I wasn't sure if I was cut out to be a teacher. I have been told by many of the students and families that my success as a teacher stemmed from my passion for the subject. Not only did I enjoy sharing my love for math, but I truly wanted each student to succeed. I knew that not all students would have an aptitude for math, but I wanted them all to at least appreciate its logic and order and usefulness in technology and every-day life. My favorite class was AP Calculus AB/BC as the students in that class were mostly very gifted and eager to learn. My second favorite class was pre-Algebra as I had the opportunity to try to instill a love of math in students who may not have previously enjoyed it. In addition to all the math classes from pre-algebra to calculus, I also taught chemistry and physics.

VP Finance

In 1992, my father formed a corporation to continue his consulting in the oil and gas industry. He knew I had recently left my job to raise my children and didn't want to pay an accountant to handle the business side of his affairs, so he asked me if I might be willing to handle the business side of things. I told him yes even though I had no idea how to do it. I purchased Quickbooks and learned how to use it and hired a tax attorney the first year to teach me how to do the corporate taxes. I have handled the corporate finances ever since. Although the work is fairly simple as there are only three employees, I have handled various situations over the years, such as implementing a SEP IRA plan, a health care plan, a corporate name change, a change from a C-corp to an S-corp, and the transfer of ownership. This is in addition to the normal activities of bookkeeping, payroll, invoicing, and taxes.

Hiking Enthusiast

I had to include this as it is my latest hobby. A few years ago, a friend called me and said, "Would you want to hike the Appalachian Trail with me?" I immediately replied, "Yes, what is the Appalachian Trail?" Three months later, we had purchased backpacks, light-weight equipment, and broken in our boots. We hiked 200 miles in a little over two weeks and were talked into signing up to do the whole trail (in sections anyway!). Last year, we only hiked 8 days because of an injury but were able to complete 150 miles despite the injury. Our inspiration is Grandma Gatewood who hiked the entire ~2100 miles when she was 67 years old after telling her grown children she was "going for a walk." She walked the trail two more times, the last when she was 75 years old. My friend and I figure we have many years to complete the remaining 1750 miles!

Software Developer

Finally, we come to my new "hat". This is my favorite "hat" because it is my true passion. I worked as an engineer because I was starting out in life and needed to work. I homeschooled my children because I felt it was the best option for them. I taught math because there was a need in the community that I could fill. I handled the financial duties of my father's corporation as a favor to him. But, I code because I want to! To me, there is almost nothing as satisfying as creating a program to do what you want it to do, having it NOT do what you wanted it to do, and then figuring out how to fix it! I can work for hours and hours on a program but feel like only minutes have passed. In the words of Marc Anthony, "If you do what you love, you'll never work a day in your life."

Hawaii in Review Project

TASK: Develop a code-first MVC application with multiple models and foreign keys that allows users to review technology or travel.

SKILLS: ASP.NET, MVC, HTML5, CSS3, BOOTSTRAP

EXECUTION: My application allows users to review attractions or events they encountered while visiting a Hawaiian Island. For each review, the user specifies the Hawaiian Island they visited. The user may add, update, or delete reviews as well as Hawaiian Islands. In addition to being able to view condensed or detailed reviews, I added a feature where all the detailed reviews can be viewed at once. In addition, I prevented the user from deleting any of the main eight Hawaiian Islands so that they would be accessible to all users in the drop-down menu.

Bank Account Project

TASK: Develop a console application to manage one client's bank account. You may hard-code the client into the system with only 1 checking and 1 savings account.

SKILLS:C#, OOP, Inheritance & Polymorphism

EXECUTION: I designed the application with a client class and an account class. The account class was a base class with derived classes for checking and savings accounts. I hard-coded 4 existing clients with account balances, but also allowed new clients to login and set up accounts. The program detects whether the user is an existing client or a new client and greets them appropriately. The client may open accounts, view balances, and deposit or withdraw funds. I added an overdraft feature where the client may withdraw more than the checking account balance if there is enough in the savings account to cover the deficit and still maintain a $1500 minimum balance.

Virtual Pet Project

TASK: Create a console application for an interactive game-like virtual pet program.

SKILLS: C# (focus on methods)

EXECUTION: My site allows the pet owner to give their pet normal care (e.g., food, water, bathroom breaks, exercise, and vet care). The pets status changes based on passing time (e.g., the pet gets hungrier as time passes), care provided by the owner, and some random events. The owner attempts to keep the pet's status in each category at an appropriate level. An overall owner score is kept which reflects the owner's skill at caring for their pet. If the pet is not taken care of properly, the program ends with the pet being given a new home.
